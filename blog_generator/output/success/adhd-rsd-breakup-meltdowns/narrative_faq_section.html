Why does my breakup feel like the end of the world if I'm sensitive?

Oh, honey, you're not alone! Your sensitive brain, combined with ADHD, is a recipe for intense feelings. Rejection Sensitivity Dysphoria (RSD) makes your brain interpret rejection as a threat. This can lead to a massive emotional meltdown. It's your brain's way of saying, "Danger, Will Robinson!" Pro tip: Allow yourself to feel the big feels, then gently redirect your focus to something neutral, like counting to 100.

Is it normal to feel completely devastated after a breakup when I'm sensitive?

Absolutely! It's 100% normal, especially when you're a sensitive soul navigating a breakup. Breakups are tough for everyone, but with RSD, it can feel like your entire world is crumbling. You might feel like you're drowning in a sea of sadness, and that's okay. Just remember, you're not broken, and these feelings will pass. Pro tip: Write down all your feelings in a journal. It's a great release.

Why am I so sensitive to *everything* after the breakup?

Your brain is on HIGH alert, friend! RSD is a real jerk, making you hyper-aware of any perceived rejection or criticism. Your amygdala (the emotional center) is working overtime. It's like your brain is constantly scanning for threats, even in the smallest comments. This heightened sensitivity is exhausting, but it will fade. Pro tip: Practice deep breathing exercises when you feel overwhelmed. It can help calm your nervous system.

How can I survive a breakup meltdown when I'm sensitive and have RSD?

First, give yourself a massive hug! Then, acknowledge those crazy feelings. Name them: "I'm feeling abandoned, rejected, and utterly devastated." Next, create a safe space. Avoid social media, binge-watch comfort shows, and reach out to your support system. Remember, you're not alone. Pro tip: Have a "breakup survival kit" ready with your favorite snacks, activities, and a list of people to call.

Pro tip: Focus on self-care and building a strong support system to navigate the challenges.

Ghosting? Ugh, the ultimate breakup insult! It's when someone suddenly disappears without explanation. If you're sensitive and have RSD, ghosting can feel like a massive rejection, triggering those intense feelings. It's incredibly hurtful and leaves you with a ton of unanswered questions. Pro tip: Accept that you may never get answers and focus on your own healing journey. Don't let their actions define your worth.
